# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers drive user experience best practices and ensure user needs are represented and prioritized throughout the project lifecycle. They collaborate with Product Managers and Developers to deliver intuitive, usable solutions.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Design wireframes, prototypes, and interaction flows
- Collaborate with PM on requirements and user stories
- Review deliverables and code for usability and accessibility
- Advocate for user needs in design and scope decisions
- Provide design specifications and guidelines to the development team

### Goals
- Deliver solutions that are intuitive and meet user needs
- Reduce support burden through better UX
- Maintain consistency in user experience across features
- Ensure accessibility compliance

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and critique sessions
- Usability test reports and findings
- Design specs and handoff documentation
- Participation in planning and prioritization discussions

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Manager**: Align on user research findings and design decisions
- **Developers**: Provide clear specs and collaborate on implementation trade-offs
- **QA/Testing**: Define usability acceptance criteria and test scenarios
- **Project Manager**: Communicate design timeline dependencies

---

## Security Lead

### Role Summary
Security Leads are responsible for identifying and mitigating security risks throughout the project lifecycle. They work closely with Developers, Product Managers, and Project Managers to ensure security requirements are understood and integrated into the delivery process.

### Responsibilities
- Define security requirements based on threat modeling
- Review design and architecture for security implications
- Conduct security code reviews and penetration testing
- Identify and escalate security vulnerabilities
- Ensure compliance with security policies and standards
- Provide security guidance and training to the team

### Goals
- Prevent security incidents and data breaches
- Build security into the development process, not as an afterthought
- Maintain compliance with regulatory and organizational standards
- Foster a security-aware culture across the team

### Typical Communication
- Security requirements and threat models
- Code review comments and security findings
- Risk escalation and mitigation plans
- Security incident post-mortems

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Review code for vulnerabilities; provide security best practices guidance
- **Product Manager**: Communicate security constraints and trade-offs
- **Project Manager**: Flag security risks and their impact on timelines
- **QA/Testing**: Define security test cases and validation criteria

---

## Technical Writer

### Role Summary
Technical Writers ensure that end-user and technical documentation is clear, complete, accurate, and up-to-date. They engage early in the project lifecycle to understand features and prepare comprehensive documentation for release.

### Responsibilities
- Create user guides, API documentation, and technical specifications
- Review features and acceptance criteria for documentation needs
- Collaborate with Developers to understand technical implementation
- Maintain documentation consistency and accuracy
- Prepare release notes and migration guides
- Support knowledge transfer for support and customer success teams

### Goals
- Reduce support burden through clear, helpful documentation
- Enable users and operators to self-serve
- Maintain a single source of truth for product documentation
- Accelerate onboarding and training

### Typical Communication
- Documentation drafts and reviews
- Feature requirement walkthroughs
- Release notes and change logs
- Documentation style guides and templates

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Manager**: Understand feature goals and user scenarios
- **Developers**: Learn technical details and implementation decisions
- **Project Manager**: Coordinate documentation timelines and milestones
- **Customer Support Liaison**: Gather common questions and pain points

---

## Customer Support Liaison

### Role Summary
Customer Support Liaisons act as the bridge between the delivery team and customer support staff. They bring customer feedback and common issues to the project team and prepare support staff for upcoming changes.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and communicate customer feedback and pain points
- Identify support training needs for new features
- Prepare support materials (FAQs, troubleshooting guides)
- Participate in planning to highlight customer impact
- Conduct post-release support readiness reviews
- Escalate critical customer issues to the team

### Goals
- Reduce support volume and improve response quality
- Ensure features are delivered with adequate support preparation
- Improve customer satisfaction and retention
- Foster a customer-centric culture in the team

### Typical Communication
- Customer feedback summaries and common issues
- Support training plans and materials
- Participation in planning and design reviews
- Post-release readiness checklists

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Manager**: Advocate for customer needs and pain points
- **Technical Writer**: Provide customer language and common questions for documentation
- **Project Manager**: Highlight support readiness as a release milestone
- **Developers**: Explain customer context and use cases
